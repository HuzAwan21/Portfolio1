In the following we will present the different possible extensions to the project, which we discussed in our group
regarding different aspects like UI, privacy and an extension for the communities.
First of all, we thought the user experience would benefit from a message pop-up whenever a user joins/leaves a community.
Since it is already implemented the way, that a boolean and a string are returned by these functions, one would just need to adapt the frontend.
Moreover, one could add visible functionality to the Like / Dislike / Approve buttons.
Given that users can already see users with the bullshitter rank, it makes sense to offer similar transparency for experts (rank Super Pro or above) or for viewing
one's followers and follows.
Speaking of bullshitters: you could very easily ruin the reputation of a person by simply naming yourself as the person and
then write a lot of bullshit. This could be fixed by a verification (that is not purchasable like the one from Twitter nowadays)
with an official document (e.g. an ID card) (bank systems nowadays do this all the time).
We also considered extending the social network to support Clubs, in addition to Communities. In a Community, all members can view posts and those
posts are also publicly visible, meaning anyone — not just members — can access them.
In contrast, Clubs are intended to be private spaces for like-minded individuals within a shared expertise area. Posts within a Club
are visible only to its members. This concept emerged from the need to provide a platform where users can discuss unverified theories or 
speculative ideas that may not yet be accepted by mainstream science and which might otherwise be flagged as bullshit by the AI, since theories are
not established facts.
To support the scientific discourse within the Clubs, only users with the rank “Super Pro” or higher are eligible to join Clubs. Within these Clubs:
Users are not penalized with negative fame for expressing unproven or unconventional ideas.
Additionally, by restricting these speculative discussions to Clubs, we prevent exposing non-experts to unverified theories, which could lead
to misinterpretations or the spread of misinformation. At the same time, this approach offers a valuable platform for users to present and discuss their ideas
among qualified peers, receiving thoughtful feedback and responses without fear of content flagging by the AI.
However, to maintain the integrity of Clubs and prevent misuse or the spread of harmful content, we introduce a community-based moderation
system: a votekick feature. Club members can collectively vote to remove users who behave irresponsibly, share irrelevant content or misuse
the platform, ensuring Clubs remain a constructive platform.
Our initial idea was to introduce a “Theory” tag that users could activate when posting speculative or unverified content. This tag would signal
that the post represents a personal theory rather than claiming something to be a fact and the AI would treat it with a softer moderation threshold—allowing
more freedom of expression without triggering immediate censorship or penalties.
However, we quickly realized the flaw in this approach: practically, anyone could label any kind of misinformation as a “theory”, opening the door
to widespread abuse—similar to the current challenges observed on Twitter under Elon Musk’s ownership, where misinformation often hides behind the
guise of opinion or speculation. To address this, we refined the concept and developed the idea of Clubs. 
AI-optimization: "AI" sometimes Fails. Therefore, one could require from the AI that it explains to the developers in
a log for debugging / support reasons, why something was flagged as bullshit and optimize the expertise_area detection.
Even though we understand that the ability to view Bullshitters is meant to serve as a form of public accountability or shaming, we believe that it is
important to strike a balance with user privacy. To enhance this, we propose adapting the existing view Bullshitters functionality as well as our
planned extension to view all experts, so that only the users you follow (who are Bullshitters or Experts) are visible.
This approach still offers transparency but restricts wide-scale public exposure, making it more privacy-conscious. While it's technically 
possible for someone to follow every account just to bypass this limitation, doing so would require significant effort.
